Blocshop

Blocshop is the quintessential clothes shopping experience. The idea for Blocshop was supposed to be a T-Shirt only shop, however, I thought that this was a little bit bland so I had decided to make a hybrid site of an online clothing store and a T-Shirt store instead. I like to try and break boundaries with design so I decided to make this a first. While scrolling through the website, the customer can scroll through to the T-shirt section and proceed to shop for T-shirts and custom T-shirts. The focus on the flow however is on custom T-shirts for customers.

Competitive Analysis

One thing that is important with projects is the ability to do a “Competitve Analysis”. A Competitve Analysis is a part of the research process that is done in order to see what competition is out there and better focus on customer usage and User Experience. In this “Competitive Analysis” I have chosen three companies that are similar to Blocshop. The three companies are ASOS, Lids, and Timberland. Styling and Branding

For the styling and branding of this project. I chose to go with black as the dominant color. The reason why I chose this particular color for this project was because black is a tad bit bland and obviously dark. This draws attention of the user away from the sites colors and towards the colors of the many varieties of clothing and T-shirts.

As for the logo, I went with the shopping bag to represent shopping at a clothing store. I chose “Ubuntu” for the font and because I like the slight slant of the font style. This to me represents sort of an express shopping feel to the user. I chose “Open Sans” and “Hevetica” to represent the text in the website respectively.

Final Prototype

For the final high fidelity prototype, what I decided to do was to introduce the homepage to show what is on the site. Then the flow goes over into the T-shirt section. This is what the user will see when they decide to purchase a custom T-Shirt. The high fidelity prototype will show more detail of the user flow and process.
